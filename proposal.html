Executive Summary

We will build an engine that **detects companies that are hiring**, **identifies the correct decision-makers**, **proposes candidates**, and **executes AI-driven outreach**, with end-to-end traceability and strict control over credit costs.

Key Conclusion

Technically and economically feasible. Monthly base (50k job offers/month) **≈ $460** (without CRM), yielding ~ 900–1,100 enriched contacts/month at **$0.10–$0.12**/contact (email).

Design Keys

  • Free Search, Pay for Enrichment: With Lusha API, *search* does not consume credits; only *enrich* (email/phone/firmographics). Optimizes TPC.
  • Low-code Orchestration (n8n): Reproducible, observable pipelines with credit *kill-switch*.
  • Aggressive Dedupe + Cache: Avoids duplicates and unnecessary spending (company TTL 30d, contact 14–30d).
  • Two‑Pass Reveal: Email first (1 credit); phone only for Tier A or after 2–3 attempts.
  • Compliance by Design: GDPR (B2B legitimate interest), suppression, source traceability, opt‑out.

Objectives & Architecture

1. Objectives

  • Companies: detect and prioritize accounts with high *Hiring Momentum*.
  • Decision-Makers: discover and enrich the correct profiles (TA/HR/Hiring Manager).
  • Desired Profiles: AI bot to organize and clean job offer info to gain clear *knowledge* of the profile the company is seeking.
  • Candidates: automatic matching (skills, seniority, language, location).
  • Outreach: multi-channel sequences with personalized AI messages.
  • Measurement: Actionable KPIs, feedback loop for re‑rank and ROI.

2. High-Level Architecture

  • Ingestion: LinkedIn Jobs + complementary via scraping tools → n8n.
  • Normalization: cleaning + job_hash + AI extraction (must/nice skills, seniority, language, work mode).
  • Enrichment: Lusha (Search→Enrich selective) + cache.
  • Storage: Postgres 16 + pgvector; S3/GCS for *raw* data and logs.
  • Matching: rules + BM25 + embeddings (pgvector) + boosts.
  • Orchestration: n8n (rate limit, retries, circuit breaker, cost‑guards).
  • Outreach: GoHighLevel CRM (pipelines) or integration with client's CRM.
  • BI/Observability: Metabase (dashboards), logs, and alerts.

Operational Flow (7 Steps)

3.1 General View

1. Scrape Offers
2. Normalize + Dedupe
3. Classify (AI)
4. Company Enrich
5. Prospect Search (Free)
6. Selective Enrich (Paid)
7. Outreach + Feedback
3.2 Step-by-Step (Operational)
  • Scraping (Apify→n8n): execute actors; receive JSON; validate schema; calculate job_hash; save to DB.
  • AI Classification: summarize description; extract must/nice/language/seniority; normalize title; write to DB.
  • Company Enrichment: Lusha GET/POST /v2/company or prospecting/company/enrich (TTL 30d).
  • Decision-Maker Search: prospecting/contact/search (filters: dept/title/seniority/geo); save *pool* without PII.
  • Prioritization: *Hiring Momentum* + persona heuristic (TA vs Hiring Manager) + geo/sector signal.
  • Selective Reveal (Paid): prospecting/contact/enrich (1 credit/email); phone (+5) only Tier A / escalation.
  • Outreach & CRM: generate AI copy; create/update in GHL; launch sequences; record responses; feedback ranking.
3.3 Step Details (Technical + Decisions)

Step 1 — Scraping

  • Primary source: LinkedIn Jobs via scraping; complements: Google Jobs, *careers* pages, etc.
  • Apify variable cost: **$1.6/1,000 offers** + $30 base.
  • ToS Risk: use IP rotation, backoff, source diversification.

Step 2 — Normalization

  • Clean HTML → description_plain.
  • Standard fields: title, location, work_type, contract, sector, salary, poster, posted_at.
  • job_hash = hash(company_norm + title_norm + location + posted_at) for strong dedupe.

Step 3 — AI Classification

  • Extract skills_must[], skills_nice[], language, seniority, *work mode*.
  • Normalize titles to taxonomy.
  • Save *rationale* for copy (reasons for fit).

Step 4 — Company Enrich

  • Paid: **1 credit/company**.
  • Endpoints: GET/POST /v2/company or prospecting/company/enrich.
  • Cache TTL 30 days; avoids repurchases by domain.

Step 5 — Prospect Search (Free)

  • Endpoint: POST /prospecting/contact/search.
  • No cost: returns name/title/company/geo **without email/phone**.
  • Save to contact_candidates (reveal_status = none).

Step 6 — Selective Enrich (Paid)

  • Contact: POST /prospecting/contact/enrich or GET/POST /v2/person (bulk) → **1 credit** per valid contact (email).
  • Direct phone (if available): **+5 credits**.
  • Policy: Two‑Pass (email→phone) + *kill‑switch* at 95% of credits.

Step 7 — Outreach + Feedback

  • CRM for pipeline and sequences; SMTP provider for sending.
  • AI Copy with RAG (job summary + 3 reasons for fit + CTA).
  • Response webhooks → update outreach_events, recalibrate ranking weights, and *persona-based copies*.

KPIs & Risks

7. KPIs (Success Measurement)

Ingestion: offers/day, dedupe %, latency, top sources.

Enrichment: company match rate, contact list rate (pre‑reveal), reveal‑to‑send %.

Outreach: reply %, meetings, offers, **placements**.

Quality: bounce %, spam %, P95 latencies, credits/day.

Business: CPA per account/placement, time‑to‑first‑reply, revenue/placement.

8. Risks & Mitigations

Scraping blocks: alternative sources + backoff + proxies; consider agreements/licenses.

Credit overruns: **kill‑switch** + alerts + reveal in stages.

High bounces: selective verification; warm-up; A/B testing of copies.

Uneven coverage: plan B (Cognism/Dropcontact).

API changes: changelog subscription + contractual tests.

Additional Details

Data Model (Useful Summary)

companies: id, legal_name, domain (unique), linkedin_url, industry, size_band, HQ, revenue, tech_stack, last_enriched_at.

jobs: id, company_id, title_raw/normalized, description_raw, skills_must/nice, seniority, work_mode, geo, salary, posted_at, source, job_hash (unique).

contact_candidates (pre‑reveal): id, company_id, lusha_id, name, title, dept, seniority, linkedin_url, geo, reveal_status, found_at.

contacts (post‑reveal): id, company_id, email, phone, email_status, phone_type, reveal_at, source.

candidates: id, email, location, skills[], seniority, availability, cv_vector, consent_status.

match_scores: id, job_id, candidate_id, score_total, breakdown, matched_at.

outreach_events: id, entity_type, entity_id, channel, template_id, status, sent_at, reply_at.

etl_audit: runs, errors, latencies, rows.

CRM vs Dedicated Frontend

GHL as CRM: Will be used for *outreach*, *pipelines*, and sequences.

Dedicated Dashboard (Next.js/Metabase): For visualization of companies, vacancies, *matches*, credit consumption, *Hiring Momentum*, KPIs.

Compliance & Deliverability

LinkedIn/ToS: human rhythm, IP rotation, backoff, source diversification.

GDPR: B2B legitimate interest (decision-makers); candidate consent for CV; *opt‑out*; record source (“Data from Lusha [date]”).

PII: log masking; retention; *forget‑me*; *suppression list*.

Email: warmed domains; SPF/DKIM/DMARC; Tier A verification.
